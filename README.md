# GameTheoryFinalProject
Final Project for AI672

Students will work in teams of two students. Each project will model a practical application (e.g. power control, network congestion, demand side management, etc.). 

Use literature to come up with realistic utility functions that capture the optimization objective for your application. Each student will propose and analyze/simulate its own model for solving that particular problem using game models and/or learning algorithms we learned in class. You can use an existing paper as a starting base.

The team will together write the final paper that summarizes their results and compares and contrast their approaches. 

The paper should be brief, no longer than 5 pages (10 point font) IEEE conference style. Paper will be due in the day of the scheduled final exam.

During the week of the final exam, each student will meet individually with the professor for a 10 min Q&A on their paper. No slides necessary, and paper does not need to be completely finished at the time of the talk.

Project will be graded on 
a) quality of modeling, 
b) student’s understanding and 
c) contribution to the analysis and quality of the presentation in the paper.
